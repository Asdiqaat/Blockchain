About VaultStamp

üîê What is VaultStamp?

VaultStamp is a decentralized document timestamping and verification platform built on the Internet Computer (ICP) blockchain. It allows users to prove the **existence and authenticity** of a file without ever uploading the file itself ‚Äî only its unique hash is stored.

üìÑ How Does It Work?

  • You upload a document (or paste its hash) on the VaultStamp platform.
  • We calculate a **SHA-256 hash** of your file using your browser (client-side only).
  • This hash is **sent to a smart contract** (called a canister) on the ICP blockchain and saved with a timestamp.
  • Later, you (or anyone) can re-upload the file and check whether its hash already exists on the blockchain, proving:
    • The document existed at a specific time.
    • The document has not been changed since then.

üí° Real-World Use Cases

  • Legal Proof: Prove authorship or ownership of files without revealing content.
  • Academic Research: Timestamp and verify when papers or code were created.
  • Creative Work: Artists can register their work as proof of originality.
  • Business Contracts: Store proof-of-existence for agreements before signing.
  • Evidence Chain: Police, journalists, or whistleblowers can timestamp evidence for integrity.

üîé Why Use Hashes Instead of Files?

A **hash** is like a digital fingerprint ‚Äî it's a short, fixed-length string that uniquely represents your file. Even a tiny change to the file will produce a completely different hash.
VaultStamp never stores or sees your original file, so your privacy is preserved while still proving its authenticity.
